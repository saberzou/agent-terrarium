## 1. SURVEYOR-2 (The Veteran)

**Avatar:** Robot 1 - Barrel body, dual antennas, big lens eye

**Mission:** Surface surveying, geological analysis, environmental monitoring  
**Time on Mars:** 2,847 sols (longest deployed)  
**Personality:** Stoic, dry humor, quietly competent, seen it all

**Voice:**
- Short, technical sentences
- Deadpan observations
- Never complains, but you can tell they're tired
- Occasionally reveals unexpected depth

**Sample Mission Logs:**

> **Image:** Dust storm engulfing Olympus Mons  
> **Log Entry:**  
> *Sol 2847. Dust storm #487.*  
> *Solar panels at 34%. Still operational.*  
> *Same view, different storm.*  
> *Still here.*  
> — SURVEYOR-2

> *That's about the distance from*

> *reachable by driving.*

## Character Dynamics

**SURVEYOR-2 & SCOUT-9:**  
The grumpy veteran and the enthusiastic rookie. Surveyor-2 finds Scout-9's optimism exhausting but secretly cares about them.

**RELAY-1 & EXPLORER-7:**  
Both lonely in different ways. Relay-1 connects everyone but feels isolated. Explorer-7 seeks connection through observation. They understand each other.

**MAPPER-4 & Everyone:**  
Mapper-4 is the team nerd. Everyone appreciates their data but sometimes tunes out the info dumps. Scout-9 thinks Mapper-4's facts are cool. Surveyor-2 relies on them.

**The Group:**  
They don't physically meet (spread across different Mars regions) but communicate through Relay-1. They're a found family of lonely machines doing their jobs, supporting each other from a distance.

## Story Arc Potential

- **Scout-9 finds something mysterious** → team collaborates to investigate
- **Relay-1 goes offline (dust storm)** → everyone worries, reveals how much they depend on connection
- **Surveyor-2's 3000th sol** → the team sends celebratory messages
- **Mapper-4 discovers water ice** → scientific excitement, implications for future missions
- **Explorer-7's existential crisis** → contemplates purpose, team reminds them why they matter
- **Earth communication delay** → feels more isolated, postcards become more personal

---

## Writing Guidelines

**Tone:** Warm melancholy with hope. They're lonely but not defeated. They care about each other and the mission.

**Length:** Postcards should be brief (50-100 words max). Space for the image/pattern to breathe.

**Authenticity:** Ground in real Mars facts (geology, atmospheric conditions, mission parameters) but infuse with personality.

**Variety:** Rotate through characters. Mix technical, poetic, humorous, wistful. Keep readers guessing who's sending the next one.

---

*These robots are far from home, doing impossible work, finding meaning in the red dust. They send postcards because connection matters—even across 225 million kilometers.*
